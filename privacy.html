Privacy Policy — yt-pipeline

Last updated: 11 July 2026

yt-pipeline ("the app") is a private automation tool operated by an individual for personal use on a single YouTube channel that the operator owns. The app accesses only the operator's own Google/YouTube account. It does not serve, collect data from, or act on behalf of any other user.

Data the app accesses

  • Metadata of videos the app itself uploads to the operator's channel (titles, descriptions, thumbnails, scheduling).
  • Performance analytics of the operator's own channel (views, retention, click-through rate).

The app does not access any other user's videos, analytics, or personal data.

Google API scopes and why they are used

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube.upload — to publish videos produced by the app to the operator's own channel.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube — to manage the metadata, playlists, and scheduling of those videos on the operator's own channel.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/yt-analytics.readonly — to read the operator's own channel performance metrics to improve future content.

Storage and sharing

OAuth credentials (refresh/access tokens) are stored locally on the operator's own machine or server. They are never transmitted to, or stored on, any third-party service. The app has no external database and shares no data with any third party.

Limited Use disclosure

yt-pipeline's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Revoking access

The operator can revoke the app's access at any time from Google Account permissions.
